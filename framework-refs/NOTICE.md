# framework-refs copyright and attribution

## Public law (reproducible with source citation)

- Austrian federal law: published by Bundeskanzleramt via Rechtsinformationssystem (RIS). Reproduction of federal law texts with source citation is permitted under the Austrian public-law regime. See https://www.ris.bka.gv.at.

- EU law: Directives, Regulations, Implementing Regulations, Decisions. Reproducible with CELEX citation per the EU publications policy. See https://eur-lex.europa.eu.

## Copyrighted material (identifiers and titles only)

- ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and ISO/IEC 27002:2022 are copyrighted by ISO and IEC. This repository contains only control identifiers (A.5.1, A.5.2, ...) and titles as factual references. Full text must be obtained via a licensed copy from ISO, IEC, or an authorised national standards body (for Austria: Austrian Standards International).

- Third-party commercial schemes and frameworks that may appear in sources (Cyber Trust Austria label artefacts, vendor documentation, etc.) are treated as proprietary_internal unless their publisher provides an explicit licence that permits redistribution.

## Attribution format

Each fetched snapshot records its attribution in the accompanying `.meta.yaml` file, including source URL, version identifier, fetched_at timestamp, and fetch method. This forms the audit trail demonstrating that downstream uses trace back to an authoritative source at a known moment in time.
