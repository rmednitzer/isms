# Acceptable Use Policy

**P-001 Revision 1 (DRAFT, illustrative)**

*Status: draft. Owner: role:ISMS-Manager. Next review: 2027-04-29.*

## 1. Purpose

Define how Acme personnel and authorised third parties use Acme information,
information processing facilities, and associated assets, so that
confidentiality, integrity, and availability are preserved.

## 2. Scope

Applies to Acme Widgets GmbH and to all information, systems, personnel, and
third parties within the ISMS scope per `governance/context/scope-statement.md`.
Coverage includes corporate laptops, personal devices used under BYOD
arrangements, the Vienna and Linz facilities, and remote access from home
offices.

## 3. Policy statements

3.1 Personnel shall use information assets only for the performance of their
work for Acme, and shall not use those assets in a manner that violates
applicable law, contractual obligations, or ethical standards.

3.2 Personnel shall protect credentials assigned to them, shall not share
credentials, and shall report suspected compromise within 4 hours per the
incident response procedure (SOP-001).

3.3 Personnel shall not attempt to bypass technical controls, shall not
install unapproved software on managed endpoints, and shall not connect
unapproved external storage to systems handling confidential or restricted
data.

3.4 Personnel shall classify and handle information per the data
classification standard (STD-005). Confidential and restricted information
shall not leave Acme-controlled systems except via approved channels.

3.5 Acme monitors information systems for security and operational purposes.
Monitoring is proportionate, governed by P-011, and conducted in accordance
with applicable law including GDPR Art. 6 and the Austrian DSG.

## 4. Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| All personnel | Comply with this policy; report incidents and suspected violations |
| Line managers | Ensure direct reports are informed and trained per SOP-012 |
| role:ISMS-Manager | Maintain this policy; review annually |
| role:CISO | Approve exceptions per SOP-005 |

## 5. Related documents

- Parent: `governance/policy/P-000-information-security-policy.md`
- Supporting: SOP-001 incident response, SOP-012 awareness and training, P-011 logging and monitoring, STD-005 data classification

## 6. Compliance

Non-compliance is addressed per the applicable disciplinary procedure.
Exceptions require risk acceptance per SOP-005, signed by role:CISO, and
shall be reviewed at least annually.
